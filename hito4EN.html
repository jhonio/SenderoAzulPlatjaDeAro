Senya Blanca


Property of the Ensesa family and built by the architect Rafael Masó in 1924, this was the first building in the urban development of S'Agaró.



As a result of this construction, the Catalan bourgeoisie decided to build their second homes in this idyllic area of Empordà, using a style of architecture that would respect the environment and urban development that would become the ideal of the Catalan garden city. Between the years 1956 and 1979, in the gardens of Senya Blanca, which had been completed with an impressive loggia that pays homage to the Italian Renaissance, the S’Agaró Music Festival was held, and with the participation of artists such as Victòria dels Àngels, Teresa Berganza, Montserrat Caballé, the guitarist Narciso Yepes and the conductor Eduard Toldrà, the entire urbanised area gained international importance. This residence and other areas along the coastal path have been the locations for the shooting of films such as ‘Pandora’ and the ‘Flying Dutchman’ with Ava Gardner, ‘Suddenly Last Summer’ with Elizabeth Taylor, ‘Mr. Arkadin’ by Orson Welles, ‘Gulliver’s Travels’, and ‘Mar Adentro’ by Alejandro Amenábar, winner of an Oscar in 2004 for best foreign language film.
