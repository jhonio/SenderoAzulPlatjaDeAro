Camí de Ronda de S’Agaró


The Camí de Ronda, (coastal path) of S'Agaró, was built in the 1950s, and together with the urban development of S'Agaró Vell, it was declared a Cultural Asset of National interest by the Generalitat de Catalunya (the autonomous government of Catalonia). The route runs through an area that perfectly balances the beauty of nature with the equilibrium of architecture.



The Camí de Ronda of S'Agaró is a walking route that follows the course of the rocky coastline, stretching from the beaches of Sant Pol to Sa Conca. This is an exceptional area of the Costa Brava, where the granite geology gives the landscape a special appearance. Particularly outstanding in terms of nature is the rocky outcrop of great geological interest that consists of a series of un-weathered rocks revealed by marine erosion that has cleared away the results of weathering that, under normal conditions, impede or hinder the observation and study of the igneous rocks in most climate types. Thus, you can see and identify different types of igneous rocks, see the relationships between them and observe how the sea and other external geological processes have each left their mark. Culturally, the noucentista architecture used in the construction of the camí de ronda and the surrounding urban development are also worthy of note.
