Cala Vaques


This is an important point of geological interest where one can observe hydrothermal processes associated to the evolution and subsequent cooling of magmatic masses. This process involves the formation of new rocks formed by secondary minerals that are stable at lower temperatures.



You can observe episienites, metamorphic rocks which stand out for their pink or reddish colours and in which the original granite has undergone a mineralogical transformation caused by hot water and strong oxidising conditions during their formation. In addition quartz-diorite can be seen, and some embankments of black diorític porphyry affected by the phenomenon of cataclasite and which surface in the form of embankments ramified in a North-South direction. A particular feature on the rocks of this is the presence of alveoli, cups of erosion and of early bossocs (protuberances) that morphologically shape the rocks.
