Cala Pedrosa


This is the only sandy cove within easy access of the camí de ronda and it is very representative of the typical Costa Brava scenery: small coves with rocky formations where the green of the pine trees meets the blue of the Mediterranean sea.



In this cove lies the menhir of Cala Pedrosa, which forms part of the set of megalithic monuments in the county of Baix Empordà. Chronologically, it dates back to between the 3rd and 4th Millennium BC. Geologically speaking, it is an anthropomorphic block of granodiorites with feldspar megacrystals. Granodiorites are the most abundant rocks found along the camí de ronda. This is a grey, plutonic rock, made up of medium to large crystals. To the naked eye one can differentiate between the grey, translucent and very bright quartz; and other small biotite, plagioclase and feldspar crystals. At this point you can also see the house of the architect Rafael Masó, the builder and promoter of the urban development and the garden city, and you can observe the uniformity of the architecture in which the simplicity of noucentisme with certain features of typical Catalan architecture is clearly evident.
