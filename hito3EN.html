Punta d’en Pau


This is the southernmost rocky point of the coastal path, extending across a grove of white pines (Pinus halepensis) that reaches the sea.



Here we come to the Punta d'en Pau viewpoint where you can take in the breathtaking views of the Costa Brava. One outstanding feature, from a geological point of view, is the presence of a fault, which formed a small island separated from the mainland by a strait, and on which the constant action of the waves has gradually eroded the rocks broken by the plane of this fault thus forming the strait. Today, this island has become a major nesting point for many pairs of Yellow-legged gulls (Larus michahellis).
